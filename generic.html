HomeFinder: Find the Best City for You

Using Python

This web application was made with my friends as part of the Texas A&M University 2020 Datathon. Our final product was a heroku web application to gauge the best city for a user based on specified metrics and sklearn's davies_bouldin_score attribute. After gauging the optimal "home(s)" for a user, we visualized these locations and used web-scraping to display images of a user's ideal city. We won 3rd place in our category.

An Investigation of the California Wildfire Crisis

Using Python and R

I wrote a comprehensive article for DataRes at UCLA that looks at California wildfire growth over the last few years, and the role climate change has played in exacerbating the current wildfire crisis. What amazed me while writing this article was how a select few number of counties in California account for so much of the annual wildfire destruction. Retina AI Datathon

Using R

The aim of this datathon was to gather eCommerce insights and leverage analytics to diagnose business strengths in terms of customer behaviors. My team was charged with analyzing visualizations that projected customer lifetime value (LTV) over 5 year and 10 year periods. By analyzing specific quantiles of our subset, we came to the conclusion that our client (the eCommerce company in question) should target higher spenders and incentivize to those customers who fall in the top 60% of high-spenders. Using Python

Having created this algorithm I used it to recognize important characters in the famous Broadway Play, Hamilton, as well as the most frequented airports across the globe. PageRank expresses an intuitive idea: important pages are linked to other important pages. As such, I look at how often a character is mentioned by another character in Hamilton and how often a specific airport is visited in the context of departures/arrivals.
